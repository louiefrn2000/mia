Reflection

As a student and at the same time, as a future educator learning through this journey in technology/subject has changed the way I understand and experience technology in education. Instead of focusing to the traditional way of teaching, technology allows me to explore topics in more interactive and engaging ways. I can collaborate, participate in online activities, and access information instantly. This makes learning feel more real, relatable, and exciting.

One of the biggest things I gained is independent learning skills. Technology taught me how to search for answers, analyze information, and make the subject more interesting and fun. I realized that learning is not only something that happens inside the classroom—it continues whenever I am curious and willing to explore.

Technology also made collaboration easier. Even when my classmates and I are not together physically, we can still work on projects, share ideas, and communicate through online platforms.This taught me how to work with others, respect different opinions, and contribute meaningfully to group tasks.

Most importantly, learning with this subject prepared me for the modern world since, almost every aspect of life today uses technology, and being familiar with it gives me a courage to be confidence. I feel more capable of adapting to new tools, new tasks, and even new learning environments.

And lastly, this subject (Technology for Teaching and Learning) did not only help me understand lessons better—it helped me grow as a learner or as an individual. It improved my creativity, critical thinking, and responsibility. As an individual who spend most of the time using the technology, I should be more responsible or take every action I make into account. Through technology, I learned that education is not just about memorizing facts, but about discovering knowledge and building skills that will guide me in the future.
